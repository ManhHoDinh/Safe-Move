## Slide 3: Solution -- Smart Routing + AI Learning Loop

**Title:** One AI Platform. Every Flood-Affected Route Decision.

**Main Message:**
SafeMove AI is a closed-loop intelligence platform that detects flood conditions, evaluates severity using LLM reasoning, optimizes routing through Google Maps, and continuously improves through reinforcement learning and user feedback.

**Bullets:**
- **Detect:** Multi-source flood detection -- traffic cameras (CV), weather APIs, news/social media crawling, IoT water-level sensors
- **Evaluate:** LLM-powered severity assessment that reasons about flood depth, road geometry, vehicle type, and historical patterns
- **Route:** Google Maps API integration that dynamically avoids confirmed and predicted flood points, with alternative path optimization
- **Learn:** Multi-agent system that ingests driver feedback, validates flood reports, and refines detection accuracy over time
- **Report:** Automated incident reports, traffic regulation suggestions, and predictive intelligence delivered to city operators

**Visual / Diagram Idea:**
Horizontal pipeline diagram flowing left to right: DETECT (camera icon) --> EVALUATE (brain/LLM icon) --> ROUTE (map pin icon) --> LEARN (feedback loop icon) --> REPORT (document icon). Each stage connected by directional arrows with a subtle cyan glow. Below the pipeline, a curved arrow loops from LEARN back to DETECT, labeled "Continuous Improvement via RL." The entire pipeline sits on a dark background with each stage in its own card.

**Speaker Notes:**
"This is our core loop. Detection pulls from every available signal -- cameras, weather, news, sensors. Our LLM layer does not just flag 'flood detected.' It reasons: how deep, how fast is it rising, which vehicle types are affected, what is the historical drainage pattern for this intersection. That reasoning feeds directly into Google Maps routing. And the learning loop is what makes this a platform, not a tool. Every driver interaction, every municipal confirmation, every event outcome trains the system. After six months in a city, SafeMove AI knows that city's flood behavior better than any human operator."

## Slide 5: Prediction Environment

**Title:** Know Before It Floods

**Main Message:**
SafeMove AI's prediction engine fuses historical flood patterns, real-time weather data, crawled news and social media signals, and IoT sensor readings to deliver 2-to-6-hour advance warnings for flood-prone intersections -- turning reactive chaos into proactive preparedness.

**Bullets:**
- **Multi-source prediction fusion:** historical flood recurrence data + live radar/precipitation feeds + crawled news articles and social media posts mentioning flooding + IoT water-level and drainage sensors
- **2-to-6-hour advance warning window** for flood-prone intersections, with probability scoring and confidence intervals
- **Probability heatmaps** overlaid on city maps showing flood likelihood per road segment, updated every 15 minutes as conditions evolve
- **Tiered warning system** that pushes graduated alerts to drivers (app/SMS), fleet operators (API), and city officials (dashboard) based on threshold severity
- **Scenario simulation** that models "what happens if rainfall exceeds forecast by 20%" to stress-test city preparedness

**Visual / Diagram Idea:**
Horizontal timeline flowing left to right, divided into three stages: "6 Hours Before" (light blue zone, scattered low-probability indicators on city map), "2 Hours Before" (amber zone, concentrated high-probability flood areas with confidence percentages), "Now" (red zone, confirmed flooding with active rerouting paths in cyan). Below the timeline, three alert notification mockups: driver push notification, fleet operator API payload, city official dashboard alert. The visual communicates escalating certainty and response intensity.

**Speaker Notes:**
"Prediction changes everything. With two to six hours of lead time, a city can pre-position barricades, adjust signal timing before congestion builds, and push rerouting to drivers before they encounter standing water. Our prediction engine is not a single-model system. It fuses five distinct data streams -- historical patterns tell us where flooding recurs, weather data tells us when, social media and news give us ground-truth confirmation faster than any sensor network, and IoT sensors give us precise water levels. The result: probability heatmaps that update every fifteen minutes, with confidence scores that city operators can trust for resource allocation decisions."

## Slide 11: Business Model

**Title:** SaaS + API + Data Intelligence

**Main Message:**
Three distinct revenue streams -- platform subscriptions for governments, API access for enterprises, and data intelligence for insurers and planners -- creating a diversified, high-margin business with strong net revenue retention.

**Bullets:**
- **Platform Subscription (B2G):** Tiered monthly SaaS pricing based on city population and coverage area -- Tier 1 ($5K/month, cities under 1M), Tier 2 ($10K/month, 1-5M), Tier 3 ($15K+/month, megacities 5M+) -- includes Command Center, Alert Engine, Reporting Suite, and dedicated support
- **API Access (B2B):** Pay-per-call pricing for logistics companies, ride-hailing platforms, delivery networks, and insurance underwriters -- $0.002/call for basic flood-check queries, $0.005/call for optimized routing, $0.01/call for full prediction + routing + reporting bundle
- **Data-as-a-Service (B2B2X):** Anonymized, aggregated flood-traffic intelligence datasets sold to insurance companies (risk pricing), real estate developers (site assessment), urban planners (infrastructure investment), and climate researchers -- annual subscription model at $50K-$200K/year per client
- **Revenue Projection:** Year 1: $400K (2 pilot cities + early API) --> Year 2: $2.5M (8 cities + enterprise API traction) --> Year 3: $10M (20 cities + DaaS revenue) --> Year 5: $45M (50+ cities, mature API, international)
- **Margin Profile:** 75-85% gross margin at scale (software + data, minimal COGS), with LLM API costs as the primary variable cost driver, declining as we fine-tune and self-host models

**Visual / Diagram Idea:**
Top half: Three revenue stream cards side by side. Each card has an icon (government building for B2G, API bracket icon for B2B, database icon for DaaS), the revenue stream name, and pricing in cyan. Bottom half: Hockey stick revenue chart with years on X-axis and revenue on Y-axis. Key milestones marked on the curve: "$400K Y1," "$2.5M Y2," "$10M Y3," "$45M Y5." The curve is rendered in a cyan-to-green gradient. Background is dark with subtle grid lines.

**Speaker Notes:**
"Three revenue streams, each targeting a different buyer with a different value proposition. Governments pay for the platform -- the dashboard, the alerts, the reports. This is SaaS with sticky, multi-year contracts and government procurement cycles that favor incumbents once you are in. Enterprises pay per API call -- every time a logistics company checks whether a route is flood-safe, that is revenue. And our data intelligence layer monetizes the aggregated, anonymized dataset we build over time -- insurance companies will pay significant premiums for real-time flood-traffic correlation data that no one else has. The combination gives us diversified revenue, strong margins, and multiple expansion vectors."
